## Design Context

### Users
Women aged 30-55 in the Twin Cities metro area who want to transform their homes — kitchens, bathrooms, basements. They have a vision (often Pinterest-driven), they're detail-oriented, and they need to trust the person doing the work before committing. They're the decision-maker in the household for home improvement purchases. They browse on their phone but research on desktop.

### Brand Personality
Warm · craftsman · aspirational. Erik Hasselquist is a licensed MN contractor (BC808643) based in Shakopee. He's personable, professional, and detail-oriented. The brand should feel like a high-end home magazine that's also approachable — not cold luxury, not budget contractor. Think: the contractor you'd recommend to your friends because he actually cared about getting it right.

3 words: **warm · intentional · trustworthy**

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark/light hybrid theme: dark sections (charcoal `#1C2027`, dark-base `#0F1115`) alternate with light sections (cream `#F0EBE0`, stone-50 `#FAFAF8`)
- Gold accent `#C9A84C` — used sparingly as the action color and decorative accent
- Display font: **Bodoni Moda** (`--font-display`) — editorial, high contrast, luxury magazine
- Body font: **Figtree** (`--font-body`) — warm geometric sans, approachable and readable
- Handwriting font: **Caveat** (`--font-caveat`) — used for QuirkyComment personality moments
- Tone: aspirational but human. The QuirkyComment component (handwriting + gold) is the signature voice element
- Anti-reference: avoid anything that looks like a generic contractor website or cold corporate site

### Design Principles
1. **Trust first** — trust signals (license, photo, reviews) should appear before the sales ask
2. **Hierarchy over repetition** — avoid identical card grids; vary size and weight to show what matters most
3. **The handwriting voice is the differentiator** — QuirkyComments are the most memorable element; use them intentionally
4. **Dark sections are for emotional moments** (testimonials, CTA); light sections are for information (services, about, location)
5. **Every CTA should feel earned** — don't push for a quote before the user has seen the work and met Erik
